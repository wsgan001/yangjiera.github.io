About

I'm a PhD Candidate in the Web Information Systems group, TU Delft. My research lies at the intersection of user modeling, recommender systems, and crowdsourcing. My primary goal is understanding knowledge creation processes, and developping new methods and tools for accelerating the process. I tackle the research question from three aspects 1) user (worker) modeling, e.g. expertise, interests, cultural background, 2) (knowledge creation) task design, e.g. complexity, and 3) user (worker) - task recommendation. My work finds its natural applications in online recommender systems, human computation systems, and urban computing systems.

[Short Bio] I'm a member of ACM SIGWEB. I received my master degree with cum laude from TU/e, Netherlands. My master thesis was completed at Philips Research, where I worked on the problem of time series classification. Before, I received my bachelor degree from Zhejiang University, China.
